April 21, 2026 Meeting Agenda (summary focused on items affecting cycling, pedestrians and housing)

1. Meeting Overview

Tuesday, April 21, 2026 · Special Meeting (Closed Session) 3:30 PM · Regular Meeting 5:00 PM

This meeting is dominated by fiscal planning and infrastructure matters, with the Council reviewing the FY 2026/27 Capital Improvement Program strategy and a year-end budget balancing plan. A notable pedestrian-safety item—funding a study on "daylighting" at crosswalks on Caledonia Street and Bridgeway—is on the business agenda, alongside discussions on downtown resident parking, a general fund transfer policy, and the re-adoption of the police military equipment use policy. The closed session covers labor negotiations, City Manager employment, and several litigation matters including the ongoing YIMBY v. City of Sausalito housing case.

2. Topics of Interest

Pedestrian Safety

Item 3.E — Authorize Professional Services Agreement with BKF Engineers for Coloma Street Sidewalk Project - Description: The Council is asked to authorize the City Manager to execute a PSA with BKF Engineers to solicit construction bids for a sidewalk project on Coloma Street, directly improving pedestrian infrastructure. - Action requested: Consent Calendar approval (vote) - Links: - Staff Report

Item 5.A — CIP Projects Strategy for FY 2026/27 Budget - Description: Council will discuss the general strategy for Capital Improvement Program projects to be incorporated into the upcoming fiscal year budget. The active and future project lists may include pedestrian, cycling, and roadway safety projects. - Action requested: Discussion and concurrence (direction to staff) - Links: - Staff Report - Attachment 1 — Completed Project List - Attachment 2 — Active Project List - Attachment 3 — Preliminary Future Project Summary - Attachment 4 — 2026 Strategic Planning Workshop Summary - Attachment 5 — Presentation

Item 5.B — Authorize $34,680 for AB 413 Daylighting Study on Caledonia Street and Bridgeway - Description: This is a key pedestrian-safety item. AB 413 (effective 2025) requires "daylighting" — removing parking and visual obstructions near crosswalks to improve driver sight lines to pedestrians. The City is funding a study by Parametric to assess how the law affects marked and unmarked crosswalks on Caledonia Street and in downtown Bridgeway. Funding comes from Measure L (Fund 117). - Action requested: Vote to authorize additional funding - Links: - Staff Report - Attachment 1 — PSA Parametric Plus Proposal - AB 413 Full Text (California Legislature)

Item 3.G — Additional $25,000 Appropriation for 2024 Roadway Rehabilitation Project - Description: Additional funding for dig-out work on various streets including Crecienta Drive. While primarily a roadway project, rehabilitation work can affect pedestrian conditions along these streets. - Action requested: Consent Calendar approval (vote) - Links: - Staff Report

Housing

Closed Session Item C4 — YIMBY v. City of Sausalito (Existing Litigation) - Description: The Council will meet in closed session to discuss the ongoing lawsuit Yes in My Back Yard v. City of Sausalito (Marin County Superior Court Case No. CIV2300652). YIMBY Law filed this case challenging the City's housing approval practices. While no public action will be taken in closed session, this case has significant implications for housing development in Sausalito. - Action requested: Closed session discussion (no public action) - Links: - No staff report is provided for closed session litigation items. - California HCD Housing Element Information

Cycling

No agenda items directly address cycling infrastructure at this meeting. However, the CIP strategy discussion (Item 5.A) and the daylighting study (Item 5.B) on Bridgeway — a primary cycling corridor — could have indirect implications for bicycle infrastructure and safety.
