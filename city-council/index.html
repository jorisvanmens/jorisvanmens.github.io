⚓ Sausalito City Council: Upcoming Meeting

June 16, 2026 Meeting Agenda Summary

📄 Download full agenda PDF

1. Meeting Overview

Tuesday June 16, 2026 · Special Meeting (Closed Session) 3:30 PM · Regular Meeting 5:00 PM

This is a packed agenda dominated by fiscal matters — the Council will review the current-year budget and adopt the FY 2026–2027 budget — alongside several significant infrastructure and policy items. Notably, the Council will vote on adopting Objective Design and Development Standards (ODDS) to implement Sausalito's 6th Cycle Housing Element, consider whether to commission a traffic analysis of the Caledonia Street area prompted by state law AB 413, and decide how to handle a citizen initiative that would revise Marinship zoning. The consent calendar also includes multiple public works contracts for street, sidewalk, and mid-block crossing projects.

2. Topics of Interest

🚲 Cycling

No agenda items are directly focused on cycling infrastructure. However, Item 3.H (Bridgeway Mid-Block Crossing Project — Princess to Richardson) and Item 5.D (Caledonia Street / AB 413 traffic analysis) involve Bridgeway and Caledonia Street, which are key corridors for cyclists. Changes to crossings, sight lines, and traffic patterns on these streets could affect cycling conditions.

🚶 Pedestrian Safety

Item 3.H — Authorize First Amendment to PSA with SANDIS for the Bridgeway Mid-Block Crossing Project (Princess to Richardson), not to exceed $91,100 - Action requested: Consent Calendar vote to authorize the contract amendment. - This project designs a new mid-block pedestrian crossing on Bridgeway, Sausalito's primary commercial corridor, improving pedestrian connectivity and safety between the Princess Street and Richardson Street blocks. - Links: - Staff Report - First PSA Amendment - Original Contract Plus Proposal

Item 3.J — Authorize Third Amendment to PSA with BKF Engineers for the Coloma Street Sidewalk Project, $28,008 - Action requested: Consent Calendar vote to authorize the contract amendment. - This continues design/engineering work for a sidewalk improvement project on Coloma Street, directly enhancing pedestrian infrastructure. - Links: - Staff Report - Third Contract Amendment with BKF Engineers - Prior Amendments and Contract with BKF - April 21, 2026 Staff Report (background)

Item 5.D — Provide Direction on Whether to Proceed with a Detailed Traffic Analysis of the Caledonia Street Area Based on Impacts from AB 413 - Action requested: Direction to staff (no formal vote on a resolution, but Council will decide whether to authorize spending on the study). - AB 413 (the "Daylighting" law) restricts parking near intersections and crosswalks to improve pedestrian and cyclist visibility. Staff is asking whether to hire Parisi Transportation Consulting (via Parametrix) to analyze the traffic and parking impacts on the Caledonia Street area, a neighborhood commercial street. This could lead to new no-parking zones, curb extensions, or other changes that directly affect pedestrian safety. - Links: - Staff Report - PSA Parametrix plus Proposal - Presentation - AB 413 bill text (CA Legislature)

Item 3.D — Adopt Resolution Adopting SB 1 Project List for FY 2026–2027 (~$199,749) - Action requested: Consent Calendar vote. - SB 1 (Road Repair and Accountability Act) funds are used for street repairs and may include pedestrian-related improvements such as ADA curb ramps. The streets list attachment details which roads will be repaired. - Links: - Staff Report - Resolution - Streets List

🏠 Housing

Item 3.F — Waiver of Second Reading of Ordinance No. 14-2025 Adopting Title 10A (Objective Design and Development Standards — ODDS) for the 6th Cycle Housing Element - Action requested: Consent Calendar vote to waive the second reading and adopt the ordinance. - This is a significant housing-related action. Title 10A establishes the city's Objective Design and Development Standards, which are required by state law (SB 35, the Housing Accountability Act, etc.) to give developers and staff clear, non-discretionary standards for approving housing projects. The ordinance implements a key component of Sausalito's state-certified 6th Cycle Housing Element. Attachments include the full ODDS text with redlines, the Planning Commission's recommendation, and visualizations from ViewSync software showing how the standards would apply. - Links: - Staff Report - Ordinance - Title 10A ODDS Text (with redlines) - Planning Commission Resolution 2025-26 - [ViewSync Compliance Report & Visualizations](https://sausalito.granicus.com/services/legistar/download/pdf/4176084/Attachment_4_-Sample_of_Compliance_Report_and_Visualizations_from_ViewSync
