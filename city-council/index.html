April 21, 2026 Meeting Agenda (summary focused on items affecting cycling, pedestrians and housing)

1. Meeting Overview

Tuesday, April 21, 2026 · Special Meeting (Closed Session) 3:30 PM · Regular Meeting 5:00 PM

This meeting is heavily focused on fiscal planning and infrastructure, with the Council reviewing the FY 2026/27 Capital Improvement Program strategy and a year-end budget balancing plan. A significant pedestrian-safety item — funding a daylighting study for crosswalks on Caledonia Street and Bridgeway — stands out, alongside a sidewalk construction project on Coloma Street. The closed session includes notable litigation items, including the ongoing YIMBY v. City of Sausalito housing case and real property negotiations for submerged tidelands near the waterfront.

2. Topics of Interest

Pedestrian Safety

Item 3.E — Coloma Street Sidewalk Project (Professional Services Agreement with BKF Engineers) - Description: Authorize the City Manager to execute a PSA with BKF Engineers to solicit construction bids for the Coloma Street Sidewalk Project, which would add or improve pedestrian infrastructure on Coloma Street. - Action requested: Consent Calendar approval (vote). - Links: - Staff Report

Item 5.B — AB 413 Daylighting Study for Crosswalks on Caledonia Street and Bridgeway - Description: Authorize an additional $34,680 from Measure L funds (Fund 117) to study the impacts of AB 413, the state law requiring "daylighting" (removing parking/obstructions near crosswalks to improve driver visibility of pedestrians) at marked and unmarked crosswalks on Caledonia Street and in downtown Bridgeway. This is directly relevant to pedestrian safety in Sausalito's most heavily walked corridors. - Action requested: Business item — Council vote to authorize additional funding. - Links: - Staff Report - Attachment 1 — PSA Parametrix Plus Proposal

Item 5.A — FY 2026/27 Capital Improvement Program Strategy (tangentially related) - Description: Council concurrence on the general CIP strategy for the upcoming fiscal year. The CIP typically includes sidewalk, roadway, and other infrastructure projects that may affect pedestrian and cycling conditions. The active and future project lists may contain relevant details. - Action requested: Business item — Council discussion and concurrence on strategy direction. - Links: - Staff Report - Attachment 1 — Completed Project List - Attachment 2 — Active Project List - Attachment 3 — Preliminary Future Project Summary - Attachment 4 — 2026 Strategic Planning Workshop Summary - Attachment 5 — Presentation

Item 3.G — Additional Appropriation for 2024 Roadway Rehabilitation Project (tangentially related) - Description: Approve an additional $25,000 for dig-out work on various streets including Crecienta Drive. Roadway rehabilitation projects can affect pedestrian and cyclist conditions. - Action requested: Consent Calendar approval (vote). - Links: - Staff Report

Housing

Closed Session Item C4 — Yes in My Back Yard v. City of Sausalito (Existing Litigation) - Description: Conference with legal counsel regarding the YIMBY Law lawsuit against the City (Marin County Superior Court Case No. CIV2300652). YIMBY Law is a pro-housing organization that typically sues cities over alleged violations of the Housing Accountability Act or failures to comply with state housing laws. This case has direct implications for Sausalito's housing approval processes. - Action requested: Closed session discussion (no public action expected beyond any reportable action announced afterward). - Links: No staff report is publicly available for closed session items. For background on state housing law compliance, see California HCD Housing Element page.

Cycling

No agenda items directly address cycling infrastructure, bike lanes, or bicycle-specific programs. However, Item 5.A (CIP Strategy) and Item 5.B (daylighting study on Bridgeway) could have indirect implications for cycling conditions on those corridors — review the attached project lists and study proposal for details.
