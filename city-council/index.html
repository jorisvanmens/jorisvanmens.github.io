⚓ Sausalito City Council: Upcoming Meeting

April 21, 2026 Meeting Agenda Summary

📄 Download full agenda PDF

1. Meeting Overview

Tuesday April 21, 2026 · Special Meeting (Closed Session) 3:30 PM · Regular Meeting 5:00 PM

This meeting is anchored by a lengthy closed session covering labor negotiations, the City Manager position, and multiple litigation matters—including the ongoing YIMBY v. City of Sausalito housing case. In open session, the Council will set strategy for next year's Capital Improvement Program, fund a pedestrian-safety "daylighting" study for Caledonia Street and Bridgeway crosswalks, review a resident parking benefit program for downtown lots, and adopt green building standards (CalGreen Tier 1). Fiscal policy also features prominently, with a year-end budget-balancing plan and a General Fund transfer policy discussion.

2. Topics of Interest

🚲 Cycling

No agenda items directly address cycling infrastructure. However, the CIP strategy discussion (Item 5.A) and the daylighting study (Item 5.B) could touch on Bridgeway corridor conditions that affect cyclists. Residents interested in cycling should monitor those items for any multi-modal components.

🚶 Pedestrian Safety

Item 3.E – Authorize Professional Services Agreement with BKF Engineers for Coloma Street Sidewalk Project - Action requested: Consent Calendar approval to authorize the City Manager to execute a PSA with BKF Engineers to solicit construction bids for the Coloma Street Sidewalk Project. - This project would add or improve sidewalk infrastructure on Coloma Street, directly enhancing pedestrian safety. - Links: - Staff Report

Item 5.B – Authorize Additional $34,680 for AB 413 Daylighting Study on Caledonia Street and Bridgeway - Action requested: Business item vote to appropriate Measure L funds for a study of the impacts of AB 413 (California's daylighting law) at marked and unmarked crosswalks on Caledonia Street and in downtown Bridgeway. - AB 413 (effective January 1, 2025) prohibits parking within 20 feet of crosswalks to improve sight lines for pedestrians and drivers. This study will assess how implementation affects parking supply, traffic flow, and pedestrian safety in Sausalito's two most-walked corridors. - Links: - Staff Report - Attachment 1 – Parametrix Proposal - AB 413 Bill Text (California Legislature)

Item 5.A – CIP Strategy for FY 2026/27 (tangential) - Action requested: Council concurrence on the general CIP strategy for incorporation into next year's budget. - The active and future project lists may include sidewalk, crosswalk, or other pedestrian-related capital projects. - Links: - Staff Report - Attachment 1 – Completed Project List - Attachment 2 – Active Project List - Attachment 3 – Preliminary Future Project Summary - Attachment 4 – 2026 Strategic Planning Workshop Summary - Attachment 5 – Presentation

🏠 Housing

Closed Session Item C4 – Conference with Legal Counsel: Yes in My Back Yard v. City of Sausalito (Case No. CIV2300652) - Action requested: Closed-session discussion of existing litigation; no public action will be taken unless the Council makes an announcement upon reconvening. - YIMBY Law's lawsuit against the City relates to alleged violations of state housing law (the Housing Accountability Act and related statutes). The outcome could have significant implications for housing approvals and development standards in Sausalito. - Links: - California HCD Housing Element Compliance page - City of Sausalito Housing Element page

Closed Session Item C6 – Conference with Real Property Negotiators: Submerged Tidelands (Pelican Harbor Associates) - Action requested: Closed-session negotiation regarding price and terms of payment for submerged tideland portions near Pine Street, Johnson Street, and Donahue Avenue (Blocks 200 & 267). - While primarily a property transaction, Pelican Harbor Associates is associated with the Marinship waterfront area, where future development or redevelopment could carry housing implications under the City's Housing Element site inventory. - No public documents are available for closed-session items.
