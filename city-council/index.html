May 05, 2026 Meeting Agenda Summary

1. Meeting Overview

Tuesday May 5, 2026 · Special Meeting (Closed Session) 3:00 PM · Regular Meeting 5:00 PM

This is a packed agenda dominated by leadership transition, fiscal planning, and contentious housing issues. The Council will vote on appointing Elaine Forbes as the new City Manager effective July 1, review the FY 2026-2027 General Fund budget, and consider two separate appeals of denied ADU/JADU applications — both of which staff recommends denying. Other notable items include whether to pursue becoming a Charter City with a measure on the November 2026 ballot, a tobacco-sales ordinance update, and approval of an amended Measure AA transportation expenditure plan.

2. Topics of Interest

🚲 Cycling

  • Item 3.F – Adopt a Resolution Approving the Amended Measure AA Expenditure Plan (TAM)
  • Action requested: Consent Calendar vote to adopt a resolution approving the Transportation Authority of Marin's amended Measure AA expenditure plan. Measure AA is a Marin County ½-cent transportation sales tax that funds a range of projects including bicycle and pedestrian infrastructure, local road maintenance, and transit. Changes to the expenditure plan could affect future cycling-related funding allocations in Sausalito and throughout Marin.

🏠 Housing

  • Item 5.F – Adopt a Resolution Denying Appeal of JADU Application at 426 Pine Street (Project ID: AOD 2026-00040)
  • Action requested: Vote to deny the appeal and uphold staff's denial of a Junior Accessory Dwelling Unit (JADU) application. The agenda materials include the City's correspondence with the California Department of Housing and Community Development (HCD) responding to a complaint by the applicant (Jake Beyer). A related Beyer claim also appears on the closed-session anticipated litigation agenda, signaling possible legal exposure. This item is significant for residents watching how Sausalito processes ADU/JADU applications under state housing law.
