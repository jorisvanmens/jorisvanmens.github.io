Sausalito City Council

April 21, 2026 Meeting Agenda (summary focused on items affecting cycling, pedestrians and housing)

📄 Download full agenda PDF

1. Meeting Overview

Tuesday April 21, 2026 · Special Meeting (Closed Session) 3:30 PM · Regular Meeting 5:00 PM

This meeting is dominated by infrastructure and fiscal planning. The Council will discuss the overall Capital Improvement Program strategy for FY 2026/27, consider funding a study on "daylighting" (improving visibility at crosswalks) on key downtown streets, and review a resident parking benefit program for downtown lots. The closed session includes several litigation items, notably the ongoing YIMBY v. City of Sausalito housing lawsuit, as well as labor negotiations and real property negotiations concerning submerged tidelands.

2. Topics of Interest

Pedestrian Safety

Item 3.E — Coloma Street Sidewalk Project (Professional Services Agreement with BKF Engineers) - Description: Authorizes the City Manager to execute a professional services agreement with BKF Engineers to solicit construction bids for the Coloma Street Sidewalk Project — a new or improved sidewalk that would enhance pedestrian infrastructure. - Action requested: Consent Calendar approval (single vote with other consent items). - Links: - Staff Report

Item 5.B — AB 413 Daylighting Study on Caledonia Street and Bridgeway ($34,680) - Description: Authorizes additional funding from Measure L (Fund 117) to study the impacts of AB 413 — California's 2023 "daylighting" law that prohibits parking within 20 feet of marked and unmarked crosswalks to improve pedestrian visibility. The study will focus on Caledonia Street and downtown Bridgeway, two of Sausalito's most-walked corridors. - Action requested: Business item — Council vote to authorize expenditure. - Links: - Staff Report - Attachment 1 — Parametrix Proposal

Item 5.A — FY 2026/27 Capital Improvement Program Strategy - Description: Council will discuss and concur on the general strategy for CIP projects to be incorporated into the upcoming budget. The project lists (completed, active, and future) may include pedestrian, cycling, and roadway safety projects. - Action requested: Business item — concurrence/direction to staff. - Links: - Staff Report - Attachment 1 — Completed Project List - Attachment 2 — Active Project List - Attachment 3 — Preliminary Future Project Summary - Attachment 4 — 2026 Strategic Planning Workshop Summary - Attachment 5 — Presentation

Item 3.G — Additional $25,000 for 2024 Roadway Rehabilitation Project - Description: Additional funding for dig-out work on various streets including Crecienta Drive. Roadway rehabilitation projects can affect pedestrian and cyclist conditions on those streets. - Action requested: Consent Calendar approval. - Links: - Staff Report

Housing

Closed Session Item C4 — YIMBY v. City of Sausalito (Existing Litigation) - Description: The Council will meet in closed session to discuss the Yes in My Back Yard v. City of Sausalito lawsuit (Marin County Superior Court Case No. CIV2300652). YIMBY Law filed this case alleging the City's housing policies violate state housing law. The outcome could significantly affect Sausalito's housing element compliance and future housing production. - Action requested: Closed session conference with legal counsel — no public action unless announced afterward. - Links: No staff report is publicly available for closed session items.

Closed Session Item C6 — Real Property Negotiations (Pelican Harbor / Tidelands) - Description: Negotiation regarding submerged tideland parcels (Pine Street, Johnson Street, Donahue Avenue areas). While framed as a real property matter, outcomes of waterfront land negotiations can influence future development and housing possibilities along the waterfront. - Action requested: Closed session conference — no public action unless announced afterward.

Cycling

No agenda items deal directly with cycling infrastructure. However, the CIP Strategy discussion (5.A) and the AB 413 daylighting study (5.B) could have indirect implications for cycling safety on Bridgeway, which serves as a primary bicycle route through Sausalito.
