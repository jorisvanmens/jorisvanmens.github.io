July 21, 2026 Meeting Agenda Summary

📄 Download full agenda PDF

1. Meeting Overview

Tuesday July 21, 2026 · Special Meeting (Closed Session) 3:30 PM · Regular Meeting 5:00 PM

This meeting's most consequential items center on pedestrian safety improvements along Caledonia Street related to state law AB 413 (5.B), a proposed ballot measure to revise the city's business license tax for the November 2026 election (5.C), and additional funding for the Bridgeway slide repair project (3.F). The Council will also hold a public hearing on updates to the Sign Ordinance (4.A), interview candidates for the Economic Development Advisory Committee and Sustainability Commission (5.A), and handle several consent items including an encroachment agreement for improvements at Lower Crescent Avenue (3.H).

2. Topics of Interest

🚶 Pedestrian Safety

Item 5.B – Provide Direction Regarding Implementing No Parking Areas Adjacent to Marked or Unmarked Crosswalks on Caledonia Street Related to Assembly Bill 413

  • Action requested: Council direction (no formal vote on an ordinance at this stage); staff is asking the Council to choose among design alternatives for implementing AB 413, which prohibits parking within 20 feet of crosswalks to improve pedestrian sight lines.
  • This item directly affects parking supply on Caledonia Street and could change how pedestrians, cyclists, and drivers interact at intersections along this key commercial corridor.

Item 3.F – Authorize Additional $50,000 and Increased Contingency for Bridgeway Slide Repair Project Below Woodward Avenue

  • Action requested: Consent calendar approval (appropriation of funds).
  • While primarily an infrastructure/geotechnical item, the Bridgeway slide repair affects a major pedestrian and cycling corridor; road and path conditions along Bridgeway are relevant to safe passage for all modes.

🚲 Cycling

Item 5.B – No Parking Areas Adjacent to Crosswalks on Caledonia Street (AB 413)

  • Also relevant to cycling: daylighting crosswalks and reconfiguring curb space can affect bike movement through intersections and may create opportunities for bike-friendly curb treatments.
  • See links above under Pedestrian Safety.

Item 3.F – Bridgeway Slide Repair Project

  • Bridgeway is the primary north–south cycling route through Sausalito; repairs and any associated lane or path modifications during and after construction are relevant to cyclists.
