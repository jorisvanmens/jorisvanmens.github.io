June 2, 2026 Meeting Agenda Summary

1. Meeting Overview

Tuesday June 2, 2026 · Special Meeting (Closed Session) 3:30 PM · Regular Meeting 5:00 PM

This meeting is dominated by fiscal and governance matters. The Council will hold its first public hearing on whether Sausalito should become a charter city and place the question on the November 2026 ballot—a significant structural decision that could affect local control over land use, taxation, and other policies. The other major items are the review of the FY 2026–27 draft budget (including a capital improvement program update) and current-year budget appropriation requests. The consent calendar includes a street resurfacing engineering contract, a planning/building services agreement renewal, dissolution of the Disaster Preparedness Committee, and receipt of a report on the AIA Design Assistance Team program.

🏠 Housing

No items on this agenda directly propose housing development approvals, zoning amendments, or housing element updates. However, two items have indirect relevance:

  • Item 3.D — Authorize a Professional Services Agreement with 4Leaf for Planning and Building Services (up to $240,000 for FY 2026–27). Contract planning and building services directly support housing-related permit processing, including ADU applications and development review.
  • Action requested: Consent calendar approval (vote).

  • Links:

    • Staff Report
    • Attachment 1 – Professional Services Agreement
    • Attachment 2 – Presentation

  • Item 4.A — First Public Hearing on a Proposed City Charter. Becoming a charter city could give Sausalito greater local control over land-use and zoning authority, which has implications for housing policy (e.g., how the city interacts with state housing mandates). This is the first of two planned public hearings, with possible ballot placement in November 2026.

  • Action requested: Public hearing; direction to staff on whether to proceed (including a second hearing July 7 and potential ballot measure).
