1. Meeting Overview

The Sausalito City Council meets on Tuesday, April 21, 2026, with a Special Meeting (Closed Session) at 3:30 PM and a Regular Meeting at 5:00 PM, held in the Council Chambers at 420 Litho Street, Sausalito, CA 94965. The meeting is also accessible via Zoom.

This agenda is notable for a significant pedestrian safety item—a study on "daylighting" at crosswalks on Caledonia Street and Bridgeway in downtown Sausalito under AB 413. The business items also focus on capital improvement program strategy for FY 2026/27, a resident parking benefit program for downtown lots, and fiscal policy discussions. The closed session includes a housing-adjacent litigation item involving YIMBY v. City of Sausalito, signaling ongoing tensions around housing policy.

Item 5.B — AB 413 Daylighting Study at Crosswalks on Caledonia Street and Bridgeway - Description: Authorize an additional $34,680 from Fund 117 (Measure L) to support a study of the impacts of AB 413 daylighting requirements at marked and unmarked crosswalks on Caledonia Street and in downtown Bridgeway. AB 413 (effective January 1, 2025) prohibits parking within 20 feet of marked crosswalks and 15 feet of unmarked crosswalks to improve pedestrian visibility and safety. - Action requested: Business item — Council vote to authorize funding. - Links: - Agenda source: https://sausalito.granicus.com/AgendaViewer.php?view_id=6&event_id=2791 - AB 413 bill text: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202320240AB413

Item 5.A — Capital Improvement Program (CIP) Strategy for FY 2026/27 - Description: Council concurrence on the general strategy for addressing CIP projects for FY 2026/27. This may tangentially include sidewalk, roadway, and pedestrian infrastructure projects depending on what is in the active and future project lists (Attachments 2 and 3). - Action requested: Business item — Council direction/concurrence. - Links: - Agenda source: https://sausalito.granicus.com/AgendaViewer.php?view_id=6&event_id=2791

Housing

Closed Session Item C4 — Yes in My Back Yard (YIMBY) v. City of Sausalito - Description: Conference with legal counsel regarding existing litigation: Yes in My Back Yard v. City of Sausalito, Marin County Superior Court Case No. CIV2300652. YIMBY legal actions typically challenge local governments over housing approval processes, zoning restrictions, or compliance with state housing law (including the Housing Accountability Act and Housing Element requirements). - Action requested: Closed session discussion — no public action expected during closed session; any reportable action would be announced upon reconvening. - Links: - Agenda source: https://sausalito.granicus.com/AgendaViewer.php?view_id=6&event_id=2791 - Sausalito Housing Element: https://www.sausalito.gov/departments/community-development/housing-element - California HCD Housing Element compliance: https://www.hcd.ca.gov/planning-and-community-development/housing-elements

Cycling

No agenda items directly address cycling infrastructure, bike lanes, bicycle programs, or multi-use paths. However, Items 5.A (CIP strategy) and 5.B (daylighting study on Bridgeway and Caledonia) could indirectly affect cycling conditions on those key corridors, which are heavily used by cyclists.
