💬 Public Comments

PG&E Presentation

PG&E City of Sausalito Local Government Forum (May 5, 2026): A presentation covering PG&E's wildfire risk reduction strategies, including situational awareness tools (weather stations, HD cameras), Public Safety Power Shutoffs, Enhanced Powerline Safety Settings, undergrounding, vegetation management, reliability improvements, financial assistance programs, and advocacy priorities.

Pelican Harbour / Sale to Cameron Razavi

3 public comments on this topic.

  • Jacob Swartz urges the City Council to block the sale of Pelican Harbour to Cameron Razavi, citing Razavi's documented history of lawsuits, OSHA citations, civil rights complaints, code violations, and jail time as evidence he is unfit to steward the harbor.

  • Alison Deal, a 30-year Sausalito harbor resident, asks the Council to protect the wildlife habitat at the end of Johnson Street and the north side of Pelican Harbour from proposed dredging, and to require a full-time harbor master in the land lease, noting the new owner has already fired the existing one.

  • Chris Monti asks the Council to weigh Bridgeway Marina's record of fire/safety violations, unpermitted use, liveaboard limit breaches, and lack of maintenance against Cameron Razavi's application for the Pelican Harbour leasehold.

Jake Beyer – Public Comment (Not on Agenda) / JADU Appeal at 426 Pine Street

Jake Beyer notifies the City Council of his intent to file suit in Marin County Superior Court against the City on multiple causes of action including trespass, Bane Act violations, constitutional violations under § 1983, Housing Accountability Act violations, Public Records Act violations, negligence, and nuisance, and asserts that City Attorney Sergio Rudin has a disqualifying conflict of interest and should be replaced by independent counsel.

Resolution Denying Appeal of JADU Permit at 426 Pine Street: A draft resolution denying Jake Beyer's appeal of the Community Development Department's denial of a 416.75 square-foot JADU application at 426 Pine Street, finding the proposed JADU does not comply with SMC Section 10.44.080 or Government Code section 66333.

Sausalito Police Department Crime and Traffic Report (Q1 2026)

Staff Report – Police Department Crime and Traffic Report: A quarterly statistical report showing Q1 2026 data including 2 assaults, 6 auto burglaries, 12 grand thefts, 0 stolen vehicles, 141 traffic citations (a significant increase over prior years), 2,608 calls for service, 2 use-of-force incidents, 2 citizen complaints, and 6 compliments; the department also onboarded 10 new Volunteers in Public Safety.

Tobacco Sales Ordinance (Item 5A – Ordinance No. 07-2026)

8 public comments on this topic.

Support: 7 / Opposition: 1. The overwhelming majority of commenters—including public health organizations (LGBTQ Minus Tobacco, Smoke-free Marin Coalition, Parents Against Vaping, APPEAL), medical professionals (San Francisco Marin Medical Society, Prof. Ruth E. Malone), and an elected official (Fairfax Councilmember Barbara Coler)—strongly support the ordinance prohibiting e-cigarette sales and establishing minimum pricing, with several urging the Council to go further by also banning nicotine pouches or all tobacco product sales. The sole opposition comes from the California Fuels and Convenience Alliance (Bryan Lam), which argues that minimum price floors will harm small "mom-and-pop" businesses, shift consumer demand to neighboring jurisdictions or the illicit market, and reduce local tax revenue without effectively reducing tobacco use.

Consideration of Proposed City Charter

Staff Report – Charter City Consideration: A comprehensive analysis recommending the Council receive a presentation on a proposed Sausalito City Charter, provide direction on whether to proceed with the charter city process, and schedule the first public hearing for June 2, 2026, with the goal of potentially placing a charter measure on the November 3, 2026 ballot; the charter would preserve the Council-Manager form of government while securing home-rule authority over fire/life safety, contracting, taxation, elections, housing, and land use.

Attachment 1 – Charter City Toolkit (League of California Cities): A reference guide covering the history of municipal home rule in California, the additional powers available to charter cities, procedures for drafting and adopting a charter, and organizational considerations.

Attachment 2 – General Law vs. Charter City Comparison Chart: A side-by-side comparison of powers, elections, compensation, term limits, and other governance characteristics available to general law cities versus charter cities under California law.

Attachment 3 – Working Draft Proposed Sausalito City Charter: A draft charter establishing Sausalito as a charter city with Council-Manager government, home-rule authority over municipal affairs, provisions for continuity of existing laws, staggered four-year council terms, and vacancy procedures.

Art in Public Places – Resolution 4510 Repeal and Replacement

Staff Report – Art in Public Places Process Review: Recommends repealing and replacing Resolution 4510 (adopted in 2000) with an updated policy that streamlines the public art review process, modernizes the definition of artwork, and establishes clearer guiding principles, noting that no new artwork has been donated and installed on City property in 32 years under the current framework.

Resolution No. XX-2026: A draft resolution repealing Resolution 4510 and adopting a new policy for the acceptance, placement, and management of gifts and bequests of artwork to the City.

FY 2026-2027 General Fund Budget

Staff Report – FY 2026-2027 General Fund Budget: Presents two budget options—a Baseline option requiring $1.8M in additional revenue with no service reductions, and an Enhanced option requiring $2.7M that adds five new positions (Risk Manager, Capital Planning Projects Manager, Administrative Aide, Community Development Director, Permit Concierge)—driven by rising personnel/pension costs, insurance, and flat revenue sources, with the 25% General Fund reserve ($5.86M) not contemplated as a resource.

Attachment 10 – Budget Presentation: A slide deck by Finance Director Angeline Loeffler showing historical General Fund surpluses despite budgeted deficits, rising personnel and pension costs, and a projected $1.89M gap in the FY 2026-2027 proposed budget, with a tentative adoption schedule of June 16, 2026.

ADU Appeal at 27 Central Avenue (Item 5F)

Meike Stefani appeals the denial of her ADU application at 27 Central Avenue, arguing that the City failed to meet the 30-day completeness determination deadline under Government Code section 66321 (rendering the application deemed approved), that her proposed design complies with state regulations for legally non-conforming buildings, and asks for compassionate consideration given her husband's critical condition after a spinal cord injury.
