Sausalito City Council Meeting – April 21, 2026

Public Comment Analysis

Item 5A: Capital Improvement Program (CIP) Projects for FY 2026/27

Total comments: 4

  • A resident asks questions about the Spring Street traffic lights cost-effectiveness, Princess Street improvement details, and requests funding for the Cycling Master Plan.
  • A resident urges updating the Bicycle Master Plan, maintaining Princess Street funding, keeping the Napa roundabout study on schedule, reopening Langendorf Steps, planning Bridgeway improvements (Gate 6 to Harbor Drive), adopting Complete Streets lane widths for resurfacing, and implementing AB 413 daylighting citywide.
  • Sausalito Beautiful supports the $60,000 street tree replacement allocation, the City Parks Master Plan, and Cloudview Park access improvements, and highlights their ongoing partnership with the city on landscaping projects.
  • A resident supports the CIP's momentum but urges updating the Bicycle Master Plan with an urban design lens, maintaining Princess Street and Napa roundabout funding, advancing Bridgeway (Gate 6 to Harbor Drive) planning, and applying Complete Streets lane widths in resurfacing projects.

Item 5B: Implementing Daylighting on Caledonia Street (AB 413)

Total comments: 31

Support for immediate daylighting compliance: ~31 (unanimous) Support for broader urban design / landscape architect-led approach (beyond traffic engineering): ~28 Support for paint-only compliance now with broader study later: ~3

Nearly all commenters support immediate compliance with California's AB 413 daylighting law, citing the overdue state deadline and a pedestrian injury at Caledonia and Turney last year. The overwhelming majority urge the Council to go beyond a narrow traffic engineering study and instead hire a landscape architect to lead a comprehensive urban design effort for Caledonia and surrounding streets—one that can offset the ~21 lost parking spaces by creating new ones on adjacent blocks, improve aesthetics, and support local businesses. Many commenters reference the Bridgeway pedestrian improvements as a cautionary example of a traffic-engineering-only approach that improved safety but was aesthetically poor, and several business owners and the Caledonia Merchants Association express eagerness to participate in shaping solutions.

Item 5C: Resident Parking Benefit Program

Total comments: 20

Support for Option 1 (maintain existing free program): ~16 Support for Option 4 (reduced rate hybrid): ~4

The large majority of commenters strongly support Option 1—continuing the longstanding free resident parking benefit—arguing it encourages downtown visits, supports local businesses, and is especially valued by seniors. Supporters also welcome the modernization to ParkMobile and PIN-code systems as a way to reduce administrative costs. A smaller group supports Option 4 (reduced rate), arguing it would generate city revenue, improve parking availability, and encourage walking and biking while still preserving a resident benefit.

Non-Agenda / General Public Comment

Total comments: 2

  • A resident requests the City Council implement a formal ticket tracking system for constituent correspondence, citing over 40 unanswered emails to city staff regarding denied ADU/JADU applications, and argues the city's non-responsiveness violates the spirit of the Permit Streamlining Act and Housing Accountability Act.
  • A resident requests an immediate traffic safety study and stop sign installation at the intersection of Tomales and Wateree near the new MLK campus, citing dangerous conditions for students and families and requesting AB 413-compliant red zone painting at the intersection.
