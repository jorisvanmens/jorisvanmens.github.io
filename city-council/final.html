💬 Public Comments

Pelican Harbour / Sale to Cameron Razavi

Jacob Swartz urges the City Council to reject the sale of Pelican Harbour to Cameron Razavi, citing Razavi's documented history of lawsuits, code violations, OSHA citations, and jail time related to unfit living conditions and housing discrimination. Alison Deal, a 30-year harbor resident, asks the Council to protect the wildlife habitat at the end of Johnson Street from proposed dredging, prevent development on the north side of Pelican Harbour, and require a full-time harbor master in the land lease. Chris Monti argues that Razavi's record at Bridgeway Marina—including fire safety issues, liveaboard violations, lack of maintenance, and tenant complaints—should disqualify him from acquiring the Pelican Harbour leasehold.

Jake Beyer – Public Comments Not on the Agenda / JADU Appeal

Jake Beyer submitted a formal notice to the City Council announcing a forthcoming lawsuit against the City in Marin County Superior Court, alleging trespass, civil rights violations, Housing Accountability Act violations, Public Records Act noncompliance, and other claims, while also asserting that the City Attorney has a conflict of interest and urging the Council to retain independent counsel.

Meike Stefani – ADU Appeal at 27 Central Avenue

Meike Stefani disputes the City's denial of her ADU application, arguing that the entrance was clearly indicated, the dormers are within the footprint of a legally non-conforming building, and the City failed to meet its 30-day completeness deadline under Government Code section 66321, meaning the application should be deemed approved.

Tobacco Retailer Ordinance (Minimum Price / E-Cigarette Sales Ban)

7 public comments. Six comments support the proposed ordinance or urge the Council to go even further—Ruth E. Malone (UCSF professor emerita), the San Francisco Marin Medical Society, the Smoke-free Marin Coalition, Fairfax Councilmember Barbara Coler, LGBTQ Minus Tobacco, Parents Against Vaping, and Rod Lew of APPEAL all emphasize the public health benefits of restricting tobacco and nicotine product sales, with several urging a complete ban on all tobacco sales. One comment, from Bryan Lam of the California Fuels and Convenience Alliance, opposes the minimum price floor and discount prohibitions, arguing they will harm small local businesses, drive consumers to neighboring jurisdictions or the illicit market, and reduce tax revenue without eliminating demand.
