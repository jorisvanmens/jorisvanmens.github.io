💬 Public Comments

Item 5A: Capital Improvement Program Projects (CIP FY 2026/27)

4 comments:

  • Joris van Mens asks questions about the Spring Street traffic lights cost-effectiveness, Princess Street improvement details, and whether the low-cost Cycling Master Plan could be funded.
  • Kieran Culligan urges the Council to update the Bicycle Master Plan, maintain Princess Street funding, earmark stair repair funds for the Langendorf Steps, plan Bridgeway improvements from Gate 6 to Harbor Drive, adopt Complete Streets lane widths for resurfacing, and implement AB 413 daylighting citywide.
  • Carolyn Revelle, on behalf of Sausalito Beautiful, supports the $60,000 street tree replacement allocation, welcomes the City Parks Master Plan, and endorses Cloudview Park safety improvements.
  • Illina Frankiv asks the Council to update the Bicycle Master Plan, keep momentum on the Napa roundabout concept, maintain Princess Street funding, advance Bridgeway planning from Gate 6 to Harbor Drive, and apply Complete Streets lane widths in all resurfacing projects.

Item 5B: Implementing Daylighting on Caledonia Street (AB 413)

30 comments received. There is near-universal support for complying with AB 413 daylighting requirements on Caledonia Avenue, with many commenters citing the pedestrian injury at Caledonia and Turney and urging the City to paint curbs immediately rather than delay further. The dominant theme is a call to expand the scope beyond a narrow traffic engineering study, with the vast majority urging the Council to hire a landscape architect to lead a comprehensive urban design/streetscape plan for Caledonia that addresses parking loss (approximately 21 spaces) by creating new parking on surrounding streets, enhancing beautification, and supporting local businesses—contrasting this with what many described as the unattractive results of the Bridgeway safety project. A smaller subset of commenters (including Kieran Culligan, Illina Frankiv, Joris van Mens, and jenny Silva) emphasize that paint-only compliance should proceed immediately and not be held hostage to any longer-term study, while business owners such as Teri Lang, Adam Driver, Bob Woodrum, Yoshi Tome, Cristin Garroway, and Arielle Madilian stress the serious economic impact of parking loss and the need for merchant involvement in the process.

Austin Arensberg – Public Comment (Traffic Safety at Tomales & Wateree)

Austin Arensberg requests that the Council direct a formal traffic study for stop signs at the intersection of Tomales and Wateree near the new MLK campus, and immediately paint the 20-foot red zones required by AB 413 at that intersection to protect students and pedestrians.

Item 5C: Resident Parking Benefit Program

20 comments received. The overwhelming majority of commenters support Option 1 (maintaining the existing free three-hour Resident Parking Benefit Program with the new ParkMobile/PIN-code cardless system), emphasizing that the program encourages residents to visit downtown, supports local businesses, and is especially important for seniors on fixed incomes. Three commenters (Kieran Culligan, Illina Frankiv, and Joris van Mens) instead support Option 4 (Reduced Rate Hybrid Program), arguing it would improve city revenue, encourage walking and biking, and increase parking availability while still preserving a resident benefit. Carroll Covey supports continuing the program but expressed frustration with the difficulty of using pay stations as an 85-year-old senior.

Jake Byer – Public Comment (Constituent Correspondence)

Jake Byer requests the Council direct staff to implement a formal ticket tracking system for constituent emails and inquiries, citing over 40 unanswered emails to City staff regarding denied ADU/JADU applications, and argues the City's pattern of non-responsiveness violates the spirit of the Permit Streamlining Act and Housing Accountability Act.
